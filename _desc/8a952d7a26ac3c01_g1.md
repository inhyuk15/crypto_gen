For the sake of simplicity, we make no distinction between the sets F<sup>_рқ‘Һ_</sup> 2<sup>+</sup><sup>_рқ‘Ҹ_</sup> and F<sup>_рқ‘Һ_</sup> 2<sup>_Г—_F</sup><sup>_рқ‘Ҹ_</sup> 2<sup>,weinterpret</sup> those to be the same. The only difference is that we write elements of the second as tuples, while the members of the first set are bit strings corresponding to the concatenation of the two elements in the tuple. The empty bitstring is denoted _рқң–_ . The algorithms assume the byte order to be little-endian. 

The specification of the Sparkle permutation and of its various instances is given in Section 2.1. Then, we use these permutations to specify the hash functions Esch in Section 2.2 and the authenticated ciphers Schwaemm in Section 2.3. 

We use вҖң+вҖқ to denote the addition modulo 2<sup>32</sup> and _вҠ•_ to denote the XOR of two bitstrings of the same size. 

## **2.1 The Sparkle Permutations** 

Our schemes for authenticated encryption and hashing employ the permutation family Sparkle which we specify in the following. In particular, the Sparkle family consists of the permutations Sparkle256 _рқ‘ӣрқ‘ _ , Sparkle384 _рқ‘ӣрқ‘ _ and Sparkle512 _рқ‘ӣрқ‘ _ with block sizes of 256, 384, and 512 bit, respectively. The parameter _рқ‘ӣрқ‘ _ refers to the number of _steps_ and a permutation can be defined for any _рқ‘ӣрқ‘  вҲҲ_ N. The permutations are built using the following main components: 

- The _ARX-box_ Alzette (shortly denoted _рқҗҙ_ ), i.e., a 64-bit block cipher with a 32-bit key

We call such a parallel application of Alzette followed by the linear layer a _step_ . The high-level structure of a step is represented in Figure 2.1. Before each step, a sparse step-dependent constant is XORed to the cipherвҖҷs state (i.e., to _рқ‘Ұ_ 0 and _рқ‘Ұ_ 1).

### **2.1.1 The ARX-box Alzette** 

Alzette, shortly denoted _рқҗҙ_ , is a 64-bit block cipher. It is specified in Algorithm 2.4 and depicted in Figure 2.2. It can be understood as a four-round iterated block cipher for which the rounds differ in the rotation amounts. After each round, the 32-bit constant (i.e., the key) is XORed to the left word. Note that, as Alzette has a simple Feistel structure, the computation of the inverse is straightforward. 

Its purpose is to provide non-linearity to the whole permutation and to ensure a quick diffusion within each branchвҖ”the diffusion between the branches being ensured by the linear layer (Section 2.1.2). Its round constants ensure that the computations in each branch are independent from one another to break the symmetry of the permutation structure we chose. As the rounds themselves are different, we do not rely on the round constant to provide independence between them. For more details, we provide a complete description of the design choices we made when choosing this component in Section 3.3.

## **2.3 The Authenticated Cipher Family Schwaemm** 

### **2.3.1 Instances** 

We propose four instances for authenticated encryption with associated data, i.e. Schwaemm128128, Schwaemm256-128, Schwaemm192-192 and Schwaemm256-256 which, for a given key _рқҗҫ_ and nonce _рқ‘Ғ_ allow to process associated data _рқҗҙ_ and messages _рқ‘Җ_ of arbitrary length<sup>3</sup> and output a ciphertext _рқҗ¶_ with _|рқҗ¶|_ = _|рқ‘Җ |_ and an authentication tag _рқ‘Ү_ . For given ( _рқҗҫ, рқ‘Ғ, рқҗҙ, рқҗ¶, рқ‘Ү_ ), the decryption procedure returns the decryption _рқ‘Җ_ of _рқҗ¶_ if the tag _рқ‘Ү_ is valid, otherwise it returns the error symbol _вҠҘ_ . Our primary member of the family is Schwaemm256-128. All instances use (a slight variation of) the Beetle mode of operation presented in [CDNY18], which is based on the well-known duplexed sponge construction. The difference between the instances is the version of the underlying Sparkle permutation (and thus the rate and capacity is different) and the size of the authentication tag. As a naming convention, we used Schwaemmr-c, where _рқ‘ҹ_ refers to the size of the rate and _рқ‘җ_ to the size of the capacity in bits. Similar as for hashing, we use the big version of Sparkle for initialization, separation between processing of associated data and secret message, and finalization, and the slim version of Sparkle for updating the intermediate state otherwise.
